## A. INTRODUCTION — WHAT IS BEING DONE TO THE PEOPLE OF GOA

The Electricity Department of Goa, through its private contractor M/s **Digi Smart Network Pvt. Ltd.** (Jaipur), has begun installing 7,50,000 Radio-Frequency (RF) prepaid "smart" electricity meters across Goan homes. The total project cost is approximately **₹890.11 crore** (₹820 cr State + ₹70 cr Centre via the Revamped Distribution Sector Scheme). The Department says this is for "modernisation," "loss reduction," and "consumer empowerment."

But did the people of Goa ask for any of this? Did the public consent to having an RF-emitting, internet-connected, remotely-controllable surveillance device fitted onto the wall of every home — often within 2-3 feet of where children sleep and elders sit? Has any cost-benefit analysis been placed before the Goa Legislative Assembly? Has any safety study been done? Has any public consultation been held?

The answer to each question is no.

Worse, on **2 April 2026**, the Hon'ble **Union Cabinet Minister for Power, Shri Manohar Lal Khattar**, stated on the floor of the **Lok Sabha**:

> *"Pre-paid electric smart meters are not being imposed forcefully on any consumers, as it is optional."*
>
> *"Consumers are free to choose whether they want a prepaid meter or continue with the existing billing system."*
>
> *"Smart meters are not being installed forcibly anywhere in the country."*

(Reported in DD News, *Hindustan Times*, *The Tribune*, *The News Himachal*; 2-3 April 2026.)

If the Union Power Minister has himself said it is optional and not being forcibly installed, then on what authority is the Goa Electricity Department forcibly installing these meters in the absence of consumers, against their written refusal, threatening disconnection, and replacing perfectly working meters?

This document sets out — point by point, with the law and the science — why the smart meter rollout in Goa is unlawful, unsafe, unscientific, and unconstitutional, and why the Hon'ble High Court of Bombay at Goa must stay it immediately, until a properly constituted public, scientific, and legal review is completed.

## B. WHAT THE LAW SAYS — AND WHAT IS BEING VIOLATED

### 1. Section 47(5) of the Electricity Act, 2003 — Consumer Choice Is The Law

The Electricity Act, 2003, expressly preserves the consumer's right to choose his meter. **Section 47(5)** of the Act reads:

> *"A distribution licensee shall not be entitled to require security in pursuance of clause (a) of sub-section (1) if the person requiring the supply is prepared to take the supply through a pre-payment meter."*

Read with sub-section (1)(b), the Act gives the **consumer — not the licensee** — the choice between (i) purchasing his own meter, (ii) being supplied through a pre-payment meter, or (iii) hiring a meter from the licensee. **Nowhere does the Act authorise the licensee to compel a particular technology of meter onto a consumer who does not want one.** A "smart meter" is a *technology choice*, not a statutory necessity.

When Digi Smart Network's personnel arrive at a consumer's premises and replace his meter against his written refusal, they are violating Section 47(5) and committing the offence of trespass and criminal breach of trust under **Sections 329, 324 and 351 of the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023**.

The Department's claim that "postpaid smart meters" are being installed and that the prepaid mode is "optional" is itself contradicted by the Department's own internal admissions. The **Chief Engineer (Consumer Care)** of the Electricity Department of Goa has stated, in writing, that **once the postpaid smart meters have been installed across the State, they will be converted to prepaid mode**. The "optional" framing is therefore a temporal sleight of hand — the consumer is told today that prepaid is optional, while the Department has already committed in writing that the prepaid mode will be imposed once the metering infrastructure is in place and the consumer has no realistic option of reverting to a non-smart meter. Consent obtained on a misrepresentation is, in law, no consent at all. *(The Department's letter is available with the petitioners and is annexed as Exhibit __.)*

### 2. Section 53 — There Is No Indian Safety Standard for In-Home RF Emissions

**Section 53** of the Electricity Act mandates the **Central Electricity Authority (CEA)** to specify safety measures for the public, property, and personnel. The CEA has notified the *Electricity (Measures Relating to Safety and Electric Supply) Regulations, 2010*. **It has NOT notified any specific safety protocol for in-home RF/microwave emissions from smart meters.**

The Department of Telecommunications' 1/10-ICNIRP norms (notified 1 September 2012) were developed for *outdoor* mobile base stations, monitored through the TERM Cell self-certification regime, and were never extended to in-home, near-field, 24×7 pulsed RF emitters mounted on bedroom and balcony walls. Smart meters are *not* "telecom equipment" under DoT licence — they are electricity-metering devices. **There exists in India a complete regulatory vacuum for in-home RF emissions from smart meters.** No Indian regulator — DoT, TRAI, BIS, CEA, or any DISCOM — has ever published an in-situ measurement of cumulative 24/7 RF exposure from smart meters at distances of less than 1 metre, which is the typical bedside / kitchen / balcony scenario in Goa.

Yet Goa proposes to install 7.5 lakh such devices in homes — without any of the safeguards that even outdoor cell towers must satisfy. This regulatory vacuum is itself sufficient ground for the Hon'ble Court to stay the rollout under the **Precautionary Principle** as part of Indian environmental jurisprudence (*Vellore Citizens Welfare Forum v. Union of India* (1996) 5 SCC 647; *A.P. Pollution Control Board v. M.V. Nayudu* (1999) 2 SCC 718).

### 3. Section 55 — The Existing Meters Are Already "Correct"

**Section 55** requires that no licensee shall supply electricity except through "a correct meter" approved under regulations made by the CEA. The existing electronic meters across Goa are BIS-certified, Legal-Metrology-stamped, and accurate to their designated class. **Section 55 does not require, and does not authorise, the forced replacement of an already-correct, working meter with a different technology of meter.** The destruction of crores of correct, working meters across India is itself a massive environmental hazard (e-waste) and a financial loss to the public exchequer, contrary to the principle of sustainable development read into Article 21 of the Constitution by this Hon'ble Court in *Vellore Citizens* (1996).

Worse, peer-reviewed science shows that the smart meters being deployed are *not* "correct" within the meaning of Section 55. **Leferink, Keyer & Melentjev**, *"Static Energy Meter Errors Caused by Conducted Electromagnetic Interference,"* **IEEE Electromagnetic Compatibility Magazine** 5(4) (2017): 49-55, DOI 10.1109/MEMC.2016.7866234 — a peer-reviewed study by the University of Twente, replicated by VSL the Dutch National Metrology Institute, found that of nine commonly-deployed static (electronic) smart meter models tested under ordinary household loads (with dimmers, energy-saving lamps, induction stoves, and PV inverters), **five over-read by between +475% and +582%**, while two further models **under-read by approximately 31%-32%**. **Five hundred and eighty-two per cent over-read.** A meter that over-reads by 582% is, by definition, not a "correct meter" within the statutory meaning. The Department has produced no IEC 61000-4 conducted-emissions test data showing that the meters being deployed in Goa do not suffer from this defect.

### 4. Section 61 — Tariffs Must Safeguard the Consumer

**Section 61(d)** of the Electricity Act requires that the State Electricity Regulatory Commission's tariff principles must "safeguard consumers' interest." How is the consumer's interest safeguarded when:

- He is forced to pay the capital cost of a meter he did not want;
- He is exposed to dynamic time-of-use surcharges that did not exist before;
- His electricity can be remotely disconnected at midnight by a software glitch on a server in Jaipur;
- His existing working meter is destroyed and dumped as e-waste;
- His monthly bill, as documented across India, can jump from ₹700 to ₹7,000 — a 10× increase — with no change in consumption?

The remote-disconnect "feature" is not an abstract risk. A prepaid smart meter that exhausts its credit at midnight — whether because of a billing error, a software glitch, or a deliberate cut for a disputed dues amount — instantly becomes a death threat to:

- **Patients on home life-support equipment** — CPAP machines for sleep apnoea, oxygen concentrators, home ventilators, dialysis machines, refrigeration for insulin and other temperature-critical medication;
- **Households with overhead water tanks** filled by electric pumps — a pre-dawn cut leaves the family without water for sanitation, drinking, or medical hygiene until office hours;
- **Children studying for board examinations** in the evening, whose preparation can be terminated by a billing dispute the parents cannot resolve before the next working day;
- **Persons working from home** — including the rapidly-growing segment of Goan IT and remote professionals whose income depends on uninterrupted supply;
- **The elderly and the bedridden** in Goa's largely nuclear-family apartment blocks, who cannot navigate the prepaid recharge process or the Department's grievance window.

The Department's grievance practice — across multiple States — has been to require the consumer to **first pay the disputed amount** and only thereafter restore supply. This is not a tariff principle that "safeguards consumer interest" within Section 61(d). It is a tariff principle that **weaponises supply against the consumer** — the very mischief the Section was enacted to prevent.

In Muzaffarpur, Bihar, **Phula Devi was reportedly issued a smart-meter bill in the range of ₹31 lakh to ₹76 lakh** (sources vary). Across Uttar Pradesh, the State Electricity Distribution Company (UPPCL) was forced to issue refunds totalling **₹75 crore** after consumer protests. In Madhya Pradesh, the Leader of the Opposition, **Shri Umang Singhar**, has formally alleged that the smart meters being deployed are "spy meters" with foreign-national contractor links. In Karnataka, the **Karnataka High Court (Justice Nagaprasanna)**, in *Smt. Jayalakshmi M. v. State of Karnataka*, **WP 12535/2025**, granted an interim stay on 26 April 2025 against mandatory smart meter installation — observing that Karnataka customers were being charged ₹8,900 against ₹900 elsewhere for the same connection — and on 13 August 2025 dismissed the broader pleas on judicial-discipline grounds (a parallel PIL was already pending before a Division Bench). The 26 April 2025 reasoning on consumer-cost arbitrariness remains directly persuasive; the 13 August 2025 dismissal turned on procedural posture, not the merits of consumer choice, and is distinguishable from the present petition, which is filed in the first instance.

This is not "safeguarding the consumer's interest." This is the opposite of it.

### 5. The JERC Supply Code — Prior Notice Is Mandatory

**Regulations 6.12, 6.13 and 6.15 of the Joint Electricity Regulatory Commission for Goa and Union Territories (Electricity Supply Code) Regulations, 2018** — read with Section 55 of the Electricity Act, 2003, and the Electricity (Rights of Consumers) Rules, 2020 — require:

- The licensee must give the consumer **prior written intimation** of the date and time of meter installation (Reg. 6.13);
- The consumer has the right to be **present** at the time of installation, and the meter must be **sealed in the consumer's presence** (Regs. 6.13 and 6.15);
- The licensee must consult the consumer on the type and class of meter, which must be approved by the Commission and the CEA (Reg. 6.12).

Reports from across Goa indicate that Digi Smart Network's personnel are arriving without prior written notice, replacing meters in the consumer's absence, leaving the new meter fitted before the consumer can object, and threatening disconnection upon refusal. **Each such installation is a clear breach of statutory procedure.** The consumer is entitled, as a matter of law, to refuse such installation, file an FIR for trespass and criminal breach of trust, and approach the Consumer Grievance Redressal Forum and the Hon'ble High Court.

A foundational question must therefore be put to the State on affidavit: **Has the Electricity Department of Goa issued any Work Order, executive instruction, or written authorisation to M/s Digi Smart Network Pvt. Ltd. expressly empowering its personnel to enter consumers' premises and replace meters without the prior written consent of the consumer?** If such a Work Order exists, it is *ultra vires* Section 47(5) of the Electricity Act read with Regulations 6.12, 6.13 and 6.15 of the JERC Supply Code, and must be quashed. If no such Work Order exists, then the contractor's installers are acting on no lawful authority at all — every installation done in the consumer's absence or against written refusal is, *prima facie*, the offence of house-trespass under Section 329 of the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023, and the State has been derelict in its statutory duty of supervision under Section 142 of the Electricity Act.

### 6. The RDSS Scheme is Facilitative, Not Mandatory

The Electricity Department of Goa says the smart meter rollout is "mandated under the Revamped Distribution Sector Scheme (RDSS), 2021." This is misleading.

The RDSS is a **financing/grant scheme** approved by the Cabinet Committee on Economic Affairs on 30 June 2021, with an outlay of ₹3,03,758 crore over FY 2021-22 to FY 2025-26. It is **not a statutory enactment** under the Constitution of India and does **not** override Section 47(5) of the Electricity Act. The official scheme document speaks of *"consumer empowerment by way of prepaid Smart metering"* — *empowerment, not coercion.* The Centre's target of "25 crore smart meters by end of 2025" is a **policy aspiration**, not a legal mandate. As of December 2025, the Government of India's own Lok Sabha figures show **approximately 4.93 crore smart meters installed nationally — of which only about 1.6 crore (≈32%) are in prepaid mode** (statement of the Hon'ble Union Power Minister, Lok Sabha, 11 December 2025). The Goa rollout proposes that **every consumer in the State (7.5 lakh meters)** be installed exclusively in **prepaid mode** — an outlier even within the national programme.

Crucially, the RDSS was designed to bring AT&C losses below 15%. **Goa's AT&C losses are already approximately 9.32% (FY 2023-24, per the Government of Goa Annual Administration Report)** — well below the RDSS target. The State has no operational reason to impose the most invasive component of RDSS on its citizens. Has the Electricity Department of Goa first solved Goa's decades-old problem of 4-5 daily power cuts in many places before rolling out this fancy and harmful global digital system? Goans demand that the bigger daily power problems be solved before any of this is imposed.

Even taking the State's "loss reduction" objective at its highest, **a less-restrictive alternative is available and was never considered**. Distribution losses occur between (i) the generation/transmission supply point and (ii) the distribution transformer, and between (iii) the distribution transformer and (iv) the consumer's premise. To **measure and isolate** these losses, it is sufficient — and standard utility practice in every developed grid — to install metrology-grade smart meters at the **supply points and at distribution transformers only**, and to reconcile the transformer reading against the **aggregate** of the existing electronic-meter readings of the consumers fed by that transformer, on a monthly basis through the existing billing system. Any divergence between the transformer total and the aggregate consumer billing flags theft, technical loss, or meter tampering — at the **feeder level** — without placing a single RF-emitting, remotely-disconnectable, surveillance-capable device on the wall of any home. This is the architecture used by competent DISCOMs worldwide for **commercial loss isolation** without consumer-premises smart metering. The State of Goa has not explained — has not even addressed — why this less-restrictive, less-invasive, less-expensive alternative was rejected. Under the **proportionality** prong of *Justice K.S. Puttaswamy v. Union of India* (2017) 10 SCC 1, the State is bound to choose the **least-restrictive means** that achieves a legitimate aim. It has chosen the most restrictive means. That alone is a constitutional infirmity.

### 7. The ₹890 Crore Project Awarded to a Two-Year-Old Company With ₹1 Lakh Capital

The **Goa Pradesh Congress Committee** has formally alleged that the Goa smart-meter contract was originally tendered at approximately **₹396 crore** and ultimately awarded at **₹890.11 crore** — a more than two-fold cost escalation that, on the Congress's case, constitutes a "scam". The Department's response is that the higher figure includes GST and the Centre's RDSS Gross Budgetary Support component. Whether the differential is legitimate or anomalous is itself a matter requiring independent investigation by the Comptroller and Auditor General of India. What is established on the record is that the contractor, M/s **Digi Smart Network Pvt. Ltd.** (CIN U33100RJ2022PTC079691, registered office Jaipur), was incorporated only on **15 February 2022**, has a paid-up capital of just **₹1,00,000**, and lists three directors — Som Nath Saini, John James Cronin, and Heather Marie Peacock — yet has been awarded a critical-infrastructure contract covering 7.5 lakh consumers, the data of every Goan household, and the remote disconnect switch of every home.

The award of a critical-infrastructure contract of this scale to a 2-year-old company with ₹1 lakh paid-up capital and no published track record in Indian power infrastructure is **manifestly arbitrary** under *E.P. Royappa v. State of Tamil Nadu* (1974) 4 SCC 3 and *Shayara Bano v. Union of India* (2017) 9 SCC 1. The Congress's alleged cost escalation is, separately, grounds for an independent investigation by the Comptroller and Auditor General of India.

The people of Goa do not consent to the data of every home in the State being placed in the hands of a 2-year-old PPP firm with ₹1 lakh of paid-up capital, irrespective of the credentials or nationality of any of its directors.

Beyond the procurement irregularity, the **architecture of the smart-meter PPP** itself diffuses the State's constitutional duty across multiple private parties in a manner that is impermissible for an essential service. In the smart-meter rollout, (i) the **electricity supply** itself remains a State function (or, in some States, is licensed to private DISCOMs such as Adani, Tata Power, Torrent, BEST); (ii) the **meter is manufactured** by one private company, often without independent State Metrology certification; (iii) the **installation and maintenance** of the meter is contracted to a third private agency such as Digi Smart Network; and (iv) the **data, billing, software updates, and remote disconnect** are operated through a fourth private entity's back-end servers. Any one of these four can change its policies, raise its charges, push a software update, suffer a breach, or simply walk away — and the consumer is left chasing four different private parties through the courts to vindicate what is constitutionally an essential service obligation of the State. **The right to an essential service under Article 21 cannot be diffused into a four-cornered private contract that the consumer is not party to and cannot enforce.** The State is the constitutional duty-bearer; the consumer is entitled to deal with the State, and to hold the State responsible. The smart-meter PPP architecture is an unconstitutional abdication of that duty.

## C. THE RADIATION HARM IS REAL, IT IS DOCUMENTED, AND THE GOVERNMENT OF INDIA HAS ALREADY ACKNOWLEDGED IT

This is the section the Electricity Department will try hardest to dismiss. Let the Hon'ble Court therefore consider the science — anchored to peer-reviewed journals, government reports, and India's own regulatory instruments.

### 8. India's Own Government Has Already Admitted That ICNIRP Limits Are Not Safe

In **2010**, the Government of India constituted an **Inter-Ministerial Committee (IMC)** comprising members from the Department of Telecommunications, the Indian Council of Medical Research (ICMR), the Department of Biotechnology, and the Ministry of Environment & Forests. The IMC's Report — relied upon verbatim by the Hon'ble Rajasthan High Court in its 27 November 2012 mobile-tower order — **expressly acknowledged that EMF radiation has both thermal AND non-thermal biological effects.**

On the strength of this admission, India reduced its public RF exposure limit to **one-tenth (1/10) of ICNIRP** with effect from 1 September 2012. The Indian limits — for outdoor mobile towers — were set at **0.45 W/m² at 900 MHz; 0.90 W/m² at 1800 MHz; 1.0 W/m² at 2100 MHz**. (With effect from 6 January 2025, the Department of Telecommunications has relaxed these limits to 1/5 ICNIRP via the Tarang Sanchar portal — a regression in precaution that, in a regime where the Government's own IMC 2010 report acknowledged non-thermal biological effects, is itself a Precautionary-Principle violation.)

**The very act of adopting 1/10 ICNIRP "as a matter of abundant precaution" — and now 1/5 ICNIRP — is a direct executive admission by the Government of India that the international thermal-only ICNIRP standard is inadequate to protect public health.** The State's own historical conduct contradicts the position it will now adopt before this Hon'ble Court.

Yet **no equivalent compliance regime exists for in-home smart meters.** The DoT TERM Cell self-certification regime applies only to outdoor BTSes. Smart meters using RF Mesh (865-867 MHz ISM band, 2.4 GHz) and NB-IoT cellular emit *inside* the home — pulsed, near-field, 24×7 — and fall in a complete regulatory vacuum.

### 9. The World Health Organization Has Classified RF Radiation as a Possible Human Carcinogen

On **31 May 2011**, the WHO's International Agency for Research on Cancer (IARC), after a working group of 31 scientists from 14 countries, classified Radio-Frequency Electromagnetic Fields as **Group 2B — "possibly carcinogenic to humans"** (Baan R, Grosse Y, Lauby-Secretan B, et al., *"Carcinogenicity of radiofrequency electromagnetic fields,"* *The Lancet Oncology* 12(7) (2011): 624-626; IARC Press Release No. 208 of 31.05.2011).

**Other Group 2B agents include lead, DDT, chloroform, and engine exhaust** — all substances which India regulates with precaution. India is a WHO member State. The DoT's continued reliance on ICNIRP 1998/2020 thermal-only limits, while WHO has classified the same agent as a possible carcinogen, is the central regulatory contradiction. And the case for upgrading RF radiation to Group 2A or Group 1 has only strengthened post-2011, on the basis of the following evidence:

### 10. The US Government's Own ~$25–30 Million Study (NTP, 2018)

The US National Toxicology Program of the National Institutes of Health conducted a 10-year, approximately US$25–30 million carcinogenicity study published in November 2018 (NTP Technical Reports 595 and 596). The conclusions, verbatim:

- **"Clear evidence"** of carcinogenic activity in male rats — **malignant schwannomas of the heart**, GSM- and CDMA-modulated 900 MHz exposure.
- **"Some evidence"** of malignant gliomas of the brain in male rats.
- **"Some evidence"** of pheochromocytoma of the adrenal medulla in male rats.
- DNA damage (comet assay) confirmed in frontal cortex of mice and hippocampus of rats (Smith-Roe et al., *Environ Mol Mutagen* 61:276-290, 2020).

The Italian **Ramazzini Institute** (Falcioni L, Bua L, Tibaldi E, et al., *"Report of final results regarding brain and heart tumors in Sprague-Dawley rats exposed from prenatal life until natural death to mobile phone radiofrequency field representative of a 1.8 GHz GSM base station environmental emission,"* *Environmental Research* 165 (2018): 496-503; **PMID 29530389**) **independently replicated the NTP heart-schwannoma finding at SAR levels approximately 1,000 times lower than the lowest NTP dose**, at field strengths within the range produced by base stations and Wi-Fi routers in residential environments — i.e., the closest peer-reviewed approximation to the chronic, whole-body, low-level, in-home RF exposure scenario which a smart meter creates.

Replication across labs at lower doses is the gold standard for carcinogen identification under the Bradford Hill criteria. The science is no longer disputed. The Department's bland assertion that "smart meters are safe" cannot be reconciled with two government-funded animal carcinogenicity studies, conducted in two countries, by two independent teams, both of which found cancer.

### 11. The International Scientific Commission Has Formally Rejected ICNIRP Limits

In October 2022, the **International Commission on the Biological Effects of Electromagnetic Fields (ICBE-EMF)** — a multinational commission whose authors include former NTP Senior Toxicologist Ronald Melnick, Lennart Hardell (Sweden), Igor Belyaev, and Joel Moskowitz (UC Berkeley) — published a peer-reviewed consensus paper:

*"Scientific evidence invalidates health assumptions underlying the FCC and ICNIRP exposure limit determinations for radiofrequency radiation: implications for 5G,"* ***Environmental Health*** **21:92** (2022); **PMID 36253855**.

The paper's core finding (verbatim): The current FCC and ICNIRP exposure limits are **based on a flawed assumption that the only health effect of RF radiation is heating** — derived from short-term (40-60 minute) behavioral studies in 5 monkeys and 8 rats from the 1980s. The paper concludes these limits **"do not adequately protect workers, children, hypersensitive individuals, and the general population from short-term or long-term RFR exposures."**

**India's DoT exposure limits are explicitly derived from ICNIRP. ICBE-EMF has stated, in a peer-reviewed BMC journal, that ICNIRP's limits are based on false suppositions.** The legal predicate for invoking the Precautionary Principle is therefore squarely met. Indian environmental jurisprudence, since *Vellore Citizens* (1996) and *Nayudu* (1999), holds that "where there is a threat of serious and irreversible damage, lack of scientific certainty shall not be a reason for postponing measures to prevent environmental degradation; the burden of proof is on the actor or developer to show that his action is environmentally benign." That burden has not been discharged by the Department.

### 12. Why ICNIRP Has Nothing To Say About Smart Meters: They Pulse — They Don't Transmit Continuously

This is the technical centrepiece that this Hon'ble Court must understand. Smart meters do not emit a continuous RF signal. They emit RF in **brief, high-amplitude pulses**, 24 hours a day, 365 days a year.

In sworn testimony before the **California Public Utilities Commission** (Application 11-03-014, 2011-2012), Pacific Gas & Electric Company stated under oath:

- Average pulse rate per meter: **9,600 to 10,000 pulses per day**.
- Maximum: **190,000 pulses per day**.
- Approximately **90%** of these pulses are *not* meter readings — they are mesh-network maintenance traffic.
- Effective radiated peak power: **2.5 watts (2,500 mW)** — i.e., **2.5 times the FCC reference level** of 1 W.
- FCC compliance is judged only by the 6-minute time-average. **The lawful "average" mathematically hides peak emissions that, instant by instant, exceed the limit.**

This pulsed exposure is biologically *more* active than continuous-wave RF at the same time-averaged power. The peer-reviewed literature is unambiguous on this point:

- **Belyaev I.,** *"Biophysical Mechanisms for Nonthermal Microwave Effects,"* in *Electromagnetic Fields in Biology and Medicine* (CRC Press, 2015), Ch. 5: *"Modulation often was the factor that determined the biological response to irradiation; reactions to pulsed and CW emissions at equal time-averaged intensities in many cases were substantially different."*
- **Lai H, Singh NP**, *Bioelectromagnetics* 16(3) (1995): 207-210; **PMID 7677797** — DNA single-strand breaks in rat brain cells at SAR 0.6-1.2 W/kg; pulsed and CW both produced damage.
- **Panagopoulos DJ, Johansson O, Carlo GL,** *BioMed Research International* (2015): Article 607053.

**ICNIRP — and India's 1/10-ICNIRP derivative — is based on 6-minute time-averaged SAR from continuous-wave exposure. It was never designed to address pulsed RF.** The Petitioners are not asking this Hon'ble Court to overturn ICNIRP. They are pointing out that ICNIRP has nothing to say about smart-meter exposure.

### 13. The Hirsch Correction — Smart Meter Exposure Is Far Higher Than the Industry Claims

The Department of Power and the DISCOMs have repeatedly told Parliament and the public: *"Smart meters emit less radiation than your cell phone."* This claim is mathematically false.

Daniel **Hirsch**, senior lecturer on nuclear policy at the University of California, Santa Cruz, in his peer review of the California Council on Science & Technology's January 2011 smart-meter report (*"Comments on the Draft Report by the California Council on Science and Technology, Health Impacts of Radio Frequency from Smart Meters,"* Committee to Bridge the Gap, 21 February 2011), demonstrated that the CCST committee — which became the basis of every utility's "smart meter is safer than your phone" claim — had committed elementary mathematical errors: it compared the *peak* power of a smart meter to the *time-averaged* power of a cell phone, and mixed localized SAR with whole-body SAR.

When the same units and consistent duty-cycle assumptions are applied:

> *"The cumulative whole body exposure from a Smart Meter at 3 feet appears to be approximately two orders of magnitude higher than that of a cell phone, rather than two orders of magnitude lower."* — Hirsch, *Comments*, p. 5.

In simple terms: **smart-meter exposure at 3 feet (≈0.9 m) is 53 to 160 times the whole-body radiation exposure from a cell phone held to the head.** This correction has never been refuted in peer-reviewed literature. The CCST silently revised its calculations in subsequent versions.

### 14. The Mesh Network Multiplies The Exposure

Smart meters do not transmit point-to-point to the utility. They form a self-healing wireless mesh, in which each meter relays the data of its neighbours. A meter at the centre of a 100-meter mesh transmits not only its own ~6 daily readings to the back-haul, but also the data of ~99 other meters, plus continuous "is-anyone-there" handshake packets. Per PG&E's sworn CPUC testimony, **at least 90%** of all RF transmissions from a single residential smart meter are mesh-network maintenance, not consumption data.

The exposure a household receives is therefore not the exposure of *its* meter — it is the cumulative exposure of every meter in the relay zone. **The petitioner cannot opt out of his neighbour's transmissions.** This is uniquely problematic in Goan apartment blocks where 30-80 meters are banked together at the entry, and the local mesh can encompass several hundred. An individual flat-owner who "consents" to one meter is, by physics, consenting to the exposure of every meter in the relay zone.

### 15. The Biology — DNA Damage at Smart-Meter-Relevant Power Levels

Peer-reviewed science demonstrates DNA damage and other non-thermal biological harms at power levels far below ICNIRP thresholds:

- **Salford LG, Brun AE, Eberhardt JL, Malmgren L, Persson BRR** (Lund University), *"Nerve cell damage in mammalian brain after exposure to microwaves from GSM mobile phones,"* *Environmental Health Perspectives* 111(7) (2003): 881-883; **PMID 12782486** — at whole-body SAR as low as **0.002 W/kg (2 mW/kg) — approximately 40 times below the ICNIRP public whole-body limit of 0.08 W/kg** — two-hour GSM exposure caused albumin leakage across the blood-brain barrier and visible neuronal damage in cortex, hippocampus, and basal ganglia, persisting up to 50 days post-exposure.

## D. THE PRIVACY VIOLATION — A SMART METER IS A SURVEILLANCE SENSOR

### 21. A Smart Meter Is Not A Meter — It Is A Sensor That Watches You

A smart electricity meter is not a billing device. It is a high-resolution sensor that records, every 15 minutes (and in many configurations every 30 seconds), the precise electrical signature of every appliance, behaviour, and life-rhythm inside a home. Two decades of peer-reviewed computer-science research has demonstrated that this signature is sufficient to reconstruct, in plain language, what a household is doing minute by minute.

- **Hart GW,** *"Nonintrusive Appliance Load Monitoring,"* ***Proceedings of the IEEE*** **80(12) (December 1992): 1870-1891.** The foundational paper. Established that the energy consumption of *individual* appliances can be disaggregated from a *single* aggregate meter reading — refrigerators, kettles, televisions, induction stoves, geysers, washing machines, oxygen concentrators each have a distinct electrical fingerprint.

- **Lisovich MA, Mulligan DK, Wicker SB** (Cornell University), *"Inferring Personal Information from Demand-Response Systems,"* *IEEE Security & Privacy* 8(1) (2010): 11-20 — even basic statistical methods correctly infer occupancy, sleep/wake times, meal-preparation routines, and presence of visitors.

- **Molina-Markham A, Shenoy P, Fu K, Cecchet E, Irwin D** (UMass), *"Private Memoirs of a Smart Meter,"* ACM BuildSys '10 (2010) — without any prior training data, off-the-shelf clustering applied to smart-meter data extracted: (i) the wake/sleep cycle, (ii) when occupants were home or away, (iii) when meals were cooked, (iv) **which TV the household watched**, (v) appliance-level usage.

- **Greveler U, Glösekötter P, Justus B, Loehr D** (Germany), *"Multimedia Content Identification Through Smart Meter Power Usage Profiles,"* IKE'12 Las Vegas / CPDP Brussels (2012) — German researchers identified **the specific film a household was watching** from smart-meter data sampled at 2 Hz, by correlating power consumption with the brightness profile of the modern flat-panel TV's backlight. A 2021 follow-up in *Energies* extended this to identifying TV channels.

A smart meter on the wall of a Goan home can therefore reveal — and reveal continuously, to whomever holds the data and is permitted to query it — when you wake up, when you sleep, when you cook, when you shower, **when you pray** (Hindu pre-dawn pujas, Catholic Mass, Ramzan iftar/sehri, Sabbath observance), whether you use medical equipment (CPAP, dialysis, oxygen concentrator, home ventilator), pregnancy patterns, when the home is occupied or empty (**burglary risk**), what TV programs you watch, even **what specific film you are viewing**.

In Goa — with its high seasonal-residency profile, Portuguese-era family homes, and tourist properties — the burglary-risk dimension alone is catastrophic. Beckel et al., Kleiminger et al., and Chen et al. (multiple papers on smart-meter occupancy detection) confirm **>78% accuracy in determining whether a property is occupied or empty** from smart-meter data alone — the single strongest predictor in burglary target selection.

### 22. The 9-Judge Bench of the Supreme Court Has Already Held That This Violates Article 21

In ***Justice K.S. Puttaswamy (Retd.) & Anr. v. Union of India*** ***(2017) 10 SCC 1***, a unanimous **9-judge Constitution Bench** held that informational privacy is a fundamental right under Article 21 of the Constitution. Justice D.Y. Chandrachud's lead judgment, paragraph 180, prescribed the **three-fold test**: (i) **Legality** — the existence of a law; (ii) **Need** — a legitimate State aim; (iii) **Proportionality** — a rational nexus between aim and means, with the **least-restrictive alternative**. *Puttaswamy II / Aadhaar* (2019) 1 SCC 1 added a fourth element: **procedural safeguards**.

The Goa rollout fails on every prong:

- **Legality:** No statute mandates compulsory smart-prepaid metering. RDSS is a financing scheme. Section 47(5) of the Electricity Act *preserves* consumer choice.
- **Necessity:** The State's "AT&C loss reduction" objective is legitimate — but Goa's losses are already below 10%.
- **Proportionality:** Less invasive alternatives exist (feeder-level smart meters; opt-in residential smart meters; monthly aggregate readings; on-meter privacy-preserving aggregation). The State has chosen the **most** invasive option.
- **Procedural Safeguards:** No Privacy Impact Assessment has been published. No Data Fiduciary registration under the DPDP Act 2023. No audit framework. No grievance redressal that does not first require payment of the disputed bill.

Justice Chandrachud held expressly (para 298): *"Privacy includes at its core the preservation of personal intimacies, the sanctity of family life, marriage, procreation, the home and sexual orientation. Privacy also connotes a right to be left alone."* Smart-meter granular telemetry is paradigm informational privacy; the rollout, on the State's own record, fails the proportionality test of the largest privacy bench in Indian history.

## J. CONCLUSION

The smart-meter rollout in Goa is unlawful, unsafe, unscientific, and unconstitutional. It violates **Sections 47(5), 53, 55, and 61 of the Electricity Act, 2003, read with Regulations 6.12, 6.13 and 6.15 of the JERC for Goa & UTs (Electricity Supply Code) Regulations, 2018**. It violates Articles 14, 19, and 21 of the Constitution of India. It violates the *Puttaswamy* judgment of the 9-judge bench of the Hon'ble Supreme Court. It violates the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023. It violates the Precautionary Principle of *Vellore Citizens* and *Nayudu*. It violates the Council of Europe's Resolution 1815. It contradicts the Union Power Minister's own statement on the floor of the Lok Sabha.

The Karnataka High Court has already examined the same question and stayed the Karnataka rollout in interim (subsequently dismissing on procedural grounds that do not affect the present petition). The Supreme Court has already ordered deactivation of an RF source on health grounds. The Italian Supreme Court has accepted RF-cancer causation. French civil courts have ordered physical remediation and removal of smart meters from homes. Lloyd's of London and Swiss Re refuse to insure the consequences. The Government of India's own Inter-Ministerial Committee admitted non-thermal biological effects as far back as 2010. The Government of India's own MoEF Expert Committee Report 2011 — reviewing 919 studies — concluded EMF radiation harms birds, bees, plants, and humans. Two government-funded animal carcinogenicity studies — the US NTP and the Italian Ramazzini — have shown cancer at smart-meter-relevant exposures. 252 scientists from 44 nations, including 12 Indian scientists, have already petitioned the United Nations and the WHO.

We the People of Goa do not consent. We did not ask for this. We did not vote for this. The Union Power Minister has himself said it is not mandatory. **No Government Resolution, no executive circular, no internal Departmental target, and no PPP contract with a 2-year-old ₹1-lakh-paid-up-capital company can override Sections 47(5), 53, 55, and 61 of the Electricity Act, 2003, the Electricity (Rights of Consumers) Rules 2020, the JERC Supply Code 2018, the DPDP Act 2023, or Articles 14, 19, and 21 of the Constitution of India.** Such lawless behaviour must stop.

The Hon'ble High Court of Bombay at Goa is the only forum that can stop it. We humbly pray that this Hon'ble Court do so — and do so now.
